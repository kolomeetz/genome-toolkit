# Genetic Determinism — Limits and Caveats

This note is the **epistemic foundation** of the vault. Every Gene, System, and Phenotype note should be read through this lens.

## Core Principle

**Genetics explains WHY you are the way you are, not what's wrong with you.**

Your genome is hardware. Most of it is rewirable through behavior, environment, and intervention. The purpose of this vault is to make that rewiring informed, not to label or limit.

## Confidence Hierarchy

| Tier | Confidence | What It Covers | How to Use It |
|------|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **E1** | 85-95% | Drug metabolism, monogenic disease, population frequencies | Act on it. Share with prescribers. |
| **E2** | 60-80% | Enzyme activity, disease risk modifiers, well-replicated GWAS | Likely reliable. Monitor and plan. |
| **E3** | 30-50% | Cortisol regulation, conditional behavioral predictions | Interpret cautiously. Context matters. |
| **E4** | 15-25% | Single-study findings, limited replication | Hypothesis, not diagnosis. |
| **E5** | 5-15% | N=1 observations, animal models, speculative | For curiosity only. Do not act on this alone. |

## Rules

1. **Always state the evidence tier.** No claim without E1-E5 classification.
2. **Always state effect size** (OR, variance explained, absolute risk change). "Associated with" is not informative without magnitude.
3. **Distinguish mechanism from behavior.** A gene affecting dopamine clearance is mechanism. "You're prone to addiction" is a behavioral leap requiring many more assumptions.
4. **Genetics ≠ destiny.** 40-70% of most behavioral outcomes is environment, choice, and circumstance.
5. **Consumer genotyping has error rates.** At 600K SNPs with 99.5% accuracy, that's ~3,000 potential errors. Imputed variants have additional uncertainty (check r²).
6. **Compound gene-gene interactions** are mechanistically plausible but rarely formally tested in humans. Treat as hypothesis.
7. **Candidate gene studies** (pre-GWAS era) have high false-positive rates. Many have failed to replicate in large GWAS. Be especially skeptical of E4-E5 claims from this era.

## The Self-Forgiveness Test

Before adding a genetic explanation to the vault, ask:

> Does this explanation lead to a **strategy**, or just passivity?

If the answer is only "that's just how I am" — reframe it. Every gene note must end with **What Changes This** (the exit ramp). If nothing changes it, the note should say so explicitly, and the claim should be E4-E5 at most.

## Common Overclaims to Watch For

- "Reward Deficiency Syndrome" — not DSM-5/ICD-11, commercial conflicts
- DRD4 novelty-seeking — GWAS null at N=40K
- Exercise-D2 upregulation — shown only in clinical populations (meth users, Parkinson's)
- IL1B SSRI nonremission (OR 1.74) — single unreplicated study
- "Genetic anxiety" — candidate gene inflation; GWAS effect sizes are tiny

## When In Doubt

- Check the evidence tier
- Check the effect size
- Check if GWAS replicated it
- Ask: what would I do differently knowing this?
- If nothing: it's interesting, not actionable
